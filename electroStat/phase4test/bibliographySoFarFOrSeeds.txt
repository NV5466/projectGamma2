# Sources for the ElectroStat Seed-Library Development Report

## A. Power-quality standards

[1] IEEE, *IEEE Recommended Practice for Monitoring Electric Power Quality*, IEEE Std 1159-2019, 2019.

Use for:

* RMS voltage disturbances
* interruptions
* voltage fluctuations
* waveform distortion
* notching
* impulsive transients
* oscillatory transients
* general power-quality terminology and event classification

[2] IEEE, *IEEE Standard for Harmonic Control in Electric Power Systems*, IEEE Std 519-2022, 2022.

Use for:

* harmonic distortion
* total harmonic distortion
* nonlinear loads
* point-of-common-coupling interpretation
* steady-state voltage and current distortion

[3] IEEE, *IEEE Standard for Measurement and Limits of Voltage Fluctuations and Associated Light Flicker on AC Power Systems*, IEEE Std 1453-2022, 2022.

Use for:

* voltage fluctuation
* flicker
* short-term flicker severity
* long-term flicker severity
* limitations of the experimental ElectroStat amplitude-modulation flicker seed

[4] International Electrotechnical Commission, *Electromagnetic Compatibility, Part 4-15: Testing and Measurement Techniques, Flickermeter, Functional and Design Specifications*, IEC 61000-4-15:2010, 2010.

Use for:

* standardized flickermeter architecture
* perceptual weighting
* flicker-severity measurement
* explaining why a modulation detector is not equivalent to a standards-compliant flickermeter

[5] International Electrotechnical Commission, *Electromagnetic Compatibility, Part 4-30: Testing and Measurement Techniques, Power Quality Measurement Methods*, IEC 61000-4-30:2025, 2025.

Use for:

* repeatable power-quality measurement procedures
* sampling and measurement interpretation
* measurement-class limitations
* future field-validation requirements

## B. Fourier and spectral-analysis foundations

[6] J. W. Cooley and J. W. Tukey, “An Algorithm for the Machine Calculation of Complex Fourier Series,” *Mathematics of Computation*, vol. 19, no. 90, pp. 297-301, 1965. doi: 10.1090/S0025-5718-1965-0178586-1.

Use for:

* FFT computation
* fundamental-frequency estimation
* harmonic search
* spectral feature extraction

[7] P. D. Welch, “The Use of Fast Fourier Transform for the Estimation of Power Spectra: A Method Based on Time Averaging Over Short, Modified Periodograms,” *IEEE Transactions on Audio and Electroacoustics*, vol. 15, no. 2, pp. 70-73, 1967. doi: 10.1109/TAU.1967.1161901.

Use for:

* power spectral density
* averaged periodograms
* noise-spectrum estimation
* spectral stability across windows

## C. Filtering and robust statistics

[8] A. Savitzky and M. J. E. Golay, “Smoothing and Differentiation of Data by Simplified Least Squares Procedures,” *Analytical Chemistry*, vol. 36, no. 8, pp. 1627-1639, 1964. doi: 10.1021/ac60214a047.

Use for:

* local polynomial smoothing
* derivative estimation
* sharp-event preprocessing
* explaining the risk of smoothing or distorting narrow events

[9] F. R. Hampel, “The Influence Curve and Its Role in Robust Estimation,” *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, vol. 69, no. 346, pp. 383-393, 1974.

Use for:

* robust estimation
* median absolute deviation
* outlier-resistant event thresholds
* resistance to impulsive contamination

[10] P. J. Rousseeuw and C. Croux, “Alternatives to the Median Absolute Deviation,” *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, vol. 88, no. 424, pp. 1273-1283, 1993. doi: 10.1080/01621459.1993.10476408.

Use for:

* robust scale estimation
* limitations and efficiency of MAD
* possible future replacement of fixed MAD thresholds

## D. Exponential and modal identification

[11] Y. Hua and T. K. Sarkar, “Matrix Pencil Method for Estimating Parameters of Exponentially Damped/Undamped Sinusoids in Noise,” *IEEE Transactions on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing*, vol. 38, no. 5, pp. 814-824, 1990. doi: 10.1109/29.56027.

Use for:

* Hankel matrices
* matrix-pencil pole extraction
* damped sinusoidal parameter estimation
* complex-conjugate poles
* decay-rate and ringing-frequency estimation
* ensemble transient analysis

[12] J. F. Hauer, C. J. Demeure, and L. L. Scharf, “Initial Results in Prony Analysis of Power System Response Signals,” *IEEE Transactions on Power Systems*, vol. 5, no. 1, pp. 80-89, 1990. doi: 10.1109/59.49090.

Use for:

* applying exponential modal identification to power-system measurements
* estimating damping, frequency, amplitude, and phase
* comparison between Fourier and pole-based representations

## E. Segmentation and optimization

[13] R. Bellman, “The Theory of Dynamic Programming,” *Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society*, vol. 60, pp. 503-515, 1954.

Use for:

* optimal regional segmentation
* WaveCompare 2 boundary selection
* minimizing total regional fitting error rather than choosing boundaries greedily
